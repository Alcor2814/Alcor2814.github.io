Mars Rovers

The twin rovers Spirit and Opportunity landed on Mars in January of 2004.
Each rover lived well beyond their planned 90-day lifecycle, though Opportunity lasted as long as nearly 15 years of continuous operation.
Together they discovered evidence that Mars used to have liquid water, and was capable of sustaining microbial life for a time.
Spirit and Opportunity were preceded by Pathfinder in July of 1997 and followed by Curiosity in 2011.
Curiosity spent six years contemporaneous with Opportunity and currently coexists with Perseverance.
Curiosity was sent to discover more information about Mars environment and its potential past habitability.
Perseverance carried the Mars helicopter Ingenuity, a demonstration of powered flight on Mars, strapped to its belly.
Perseverance was sent to tests various theoretical landing techniques, scientific methods, and to study Mars' environment for potential future missions.
Perseverance has also been collecting core rock and soil samples which are intended to one day be retrieved for study!
