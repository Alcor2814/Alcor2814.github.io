Apollo Missions

The Apollo missions were 17 missions which aimed to travel beyond low Earth orbit and to the Moon.
On July 20th, 1969 Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin made history as the first humans to walk on the Moon.
The astronauts left behind an American flag, medallions honouring the crew of Apollo I, and a plaque saying "we came in peace for all mankind."
The first Apollo mission to reach space was Apollo 7, a flight beset by its crewmembers developing head colds during the mission.
The first Apollo mission to reach the Moon was Apollo 8, the mission which brought back the iconic "Earthrise" photo.
Six of Apollo's eleven crewed missions landed on the surface of the Moon.
The Apollo missions revolutionised science with every experiment, such as proving the theory that a feather and hammer fall at the same speed in a vacuum.
The Apollo spacecraft served as the basis for the later Apollo-Soyuz docking mission and the Skylab space laboratory.
Each of the above missions served as a foundation for the International Space Station.
