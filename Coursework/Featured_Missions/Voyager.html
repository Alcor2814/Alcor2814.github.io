Voyager

The twin Voyager probes were initially designed for exploration of Jupiter and Saturn.
They made many discoveries on their journey, with Voyager 2 becoming the only spacecraft to explore Uranus and Neptune.
They have since reached Interstellar space in 2012 and 2018 respectively.
Pioneers 10 and 11 preceded Voyager in leaving the solar system but Voyager 1 has since passed Pioneer 10, becoming the most distant human-made object in space.
Both Voyagers carry "golden records", a 12-inch gold-plated copper disk which contains sound and images.
Each disk contains sounds and images selected to portray life on Earth, such as musical selections and spoken greetings in fifty-five languages.
As of 2024 Voyager 1 is 163AU away from the sun, that's 163 times the distance of the Earth's orbit to the sun!
Voyager 2 is comparatively closer at 136AU from the sun.
Voyager 1 will see its 50th Anniversary on the 5th September 2028.
