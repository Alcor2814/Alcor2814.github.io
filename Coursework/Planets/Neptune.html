NEPTUNE

Furthest from the Sun:
Neptune can travel so far away from the sun that the dwarf planet Pluto's orbit can be closer for a period of 20 years.
Neptune was named for the Roman god of the sea, due to its structure and colour.
Scientists believe there might be an ocean of super hot water under Neptune's clouds, kept from boiling away by incredibly high pressure.

Orbit and Rotation:
One day on Neptune takes about 16 hours to complete.
Neptune makes a complete orbit around the sun every 165 Earth years, meaning it completed its first orbit since discovery in 2011.
Neptune rotates at an angle of 28 degrees, giving it similar seasons to Earth though across 40 year stretches.

Moons and Rings:
Neptune has 16 known moons, with each moon being named for lesser sea gods and nymphs in Greek mythology.
Triton orbits Neptune in a direction opposite to Neptune's rotation, the only moon in the solar system to do so.
Triton's thin atmosphere has been observed to be mysteriously growing warmer despite a surface temperature of -235 degrees celsius.
Neptune has at least five main rings and four prominent ring arcs, named after astronomers who discovered Neptune and its moons.

Structure:
Neptune's atmosphere is made up mostly of hydrogen and helium much like Uranus but exhibits a more vivid blue, suggesting an unknown component.
Neptune is composed of gas, water, and other melted ices.
Neptune's wind can be up to nine times stronger than Earth's, reaching speeds of up to 2,000 kilometres per hour.
