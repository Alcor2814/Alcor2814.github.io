MARS

Fourth Rock from the Sun:
Mars is approximately half the size of Earth with a radius of 3,390 kilometres. Mars is on average 228 million kilometres away from the sun, or 1.5 astronomical units. A year on Mars lasts 687 Earth Days, while each day lasts 24.6 hours.

Life and Habitation:
Scientists don't expect to find living things on Mars currently due to its lack of an atmosphere and cold temperature.
Instead, many missions are launched with the aim of discovering proof of life existing long ago or good conditions to support future life.

Structure:
Iron minerals on the surface of Mars cause it to appear reddish as a result of oxidation.
Mars has two small moons namerd Phobos and Deimos. Mars has a dense iron, nickel, and sulfur core surrounded by a rocky mantle and deep crust. Mars does appear to have had a watery past as indicated by its river networks and lakebeds.

Missions:
Mars is subject to many missions as a result of its similarity to Earth and past potential for life. The purposes of missions vary from long term studies, like the rovers, to tests of equipment, to studies of Mars from orbit. NASA plans to extend the framework created by its Artemis missions to support missions to and colonisation of Mars.
