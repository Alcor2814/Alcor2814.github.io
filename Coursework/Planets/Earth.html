EARTH

Third Rock From the Sun:
Earth is the largest of the terrestrial planets, planets composed primarily of rock or metal.
Earth sits at an average of 150 million kilometres from the sun, a distance which is the basis for the Astronomical Unit.
At a distance of one astronomical unit it takes eight minutes for light to travel from the sun to the Earth.
Earth also has an axial tilt of 23.4 degrees causing a yearly cycle of seasons affected by solar heating.

Life:
Earth is the only planet known to have life in possibly the entire universe.
Most of Earth is covered in liquid water, which provides a very convenient place for life to begin approximately 3.8 billion years ago.
Earth's mix of chemicals and temperature provide a good basis for life to develop, other planets with Earth-like qualities are typically contained in the "Goldilocks Zone" of habitability.
Earth also has an axial tilt of 23.4 degrees causing a yearly cycle of seasons affected by solar heating.

Planetary Shield:
Near to the surface Earth has an atmosphere consisting of 78% Nitrogen, 21% Oxygen, and 1% of trace other gases.
The atmosphere also protects the surface from meteoroids, many of which burn up in the atmosphere or at least reduce in size.
The planet's rapid rotation and molten nickel-iron core create a magnetic field.
The Magnetosphere has a variety of functions, such as pointing all compasses internal or otherwise North and shielding the planet from solar or cosmic radiation.

Orbits:
The Earth completes a full orbit of the sun every 365.25 days, a fact which creates "leap days" every four years.
The Earth is the only planet in the solar system with only one moon.
The Earth has an extensive array of satellites and other spacecraft orbiting it, observing both the planet and its distant surroundings.
