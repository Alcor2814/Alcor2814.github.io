VENUS

Second son of the Sun:
Venus completes an orbit of the sun in 225 Earth days, which is actually less time than a day on Venus.
A day on Venus is 243 Earth days long, and subject to temperatures as high as 475 degrees Celsius.
Venus and Uranus both rotate in the opposite direction to the other planets.

Habitability:
Venus is a strong candidate for habitability in the solar system. 50 kilometres up from the surface temperatures range from 30 to 70 Celsius.
Venus' atmosphere is known to contain linkages of sulfur atoms which could provide microbes with a coating to protect them from sulfuric acid and ultraviolet light.
Russian Venera probes did detect particles in Venus' lower atmosphere approximately a micron in length, the same size as an Earth bacterium.

Structure:
Structurally Earth and Venus are remarkably similar, each having an iron core surrounded by a hot rock mantle and a rocky crust.
Venus' surface is dotted with thousands of volcanoes and home to an extreme atmmisohere, mostly carbon dioxide based with clouds composed of sulfuric acid.
Of the Russian Venera probes the longest survivor lasted two hours in the incredibly corrosive atmosphere.

Missions to Venus:
Venus was the site of many firsts in interplanetary missions in human history, including the first interplanetary mission ever.
The Soviets launched 16 Venera probes with varying aims and success. Venus has been examined numerous times to identify its potential for life or habitability.
