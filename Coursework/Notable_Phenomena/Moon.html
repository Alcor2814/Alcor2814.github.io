The Moon

Earth's Moon is the most well-known of all moons in the universe, for obvious reasons.
The Moon was likely formed after a Mars-sized body collided with Earth several billion years ago.
The Moon gets its name from the fact that people didn't know other bodies themselves had natural satellites as well.
The Moon is less than a third of the with of the Earth, with a radius of about 1,740 kilometres.
The Moon stays at an average of 384,400 kilometres away from the Earth and continues to move away from the Earth at a rate of 1 inch per year.
The Moon rotates at the same rate that it orbits the Earth, causing it to always face the Earth with the same hemisphere.
Water was detected on the surface of the Moon in 2008, discovered by the Indian Chandrayaan-1 mission.
The temperature of the Moon varies from 127 degrees celsius to -173 degrees celsius.
The Apollo missions saw crewed missions land on the surface of the Moon for the first time in human history.
